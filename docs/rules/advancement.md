# Advancement

## Earning XP

Shadowdark awards XP for **treasure recovered**, not monsters killed — an OSR tradition.
XP is abstract; the GM assigns a tier to each treasure find:

| Find | XP Awarded (per party member) |
|---|---|
| Normal hoard     | 1 XP  |
| Extraordinary    | 3 XP  |
| Legendary        | 10 XP |

Boons, discoveries, and completing objectives may also grant XP at GM discretion.

## XP Thresholds

XP **resets to 0** after each level-up.

| Current Level | XP Needed to Level Up |
|---|---|
| 1  | 10  |
| 2  | 20  |
| 3  | 30  |
| 4  | 40  |
| 5  | 50  |
| 6  | 60  |
| 7  | 70  |
| 8  | 80  |
| 9  | 90  |
| 10 | 100 (maximum level) |

Formula: **current level × 10** XP required to reach the next level.

## What Happens on Level Up

1. Roll your class's hit die and **add the result to your maximum HP**
   — CON modifier is not re-added; it only applied at 1st level
2. Priests and Wizards gain new spells per their class's spells-known table
3. At **odd levels** (1, 3, 5, 7, 9), roll **2d6 on your class's Talent table** — the result is permanent

## Talent Rolls

Talents replace feats, ability score increases, and class power spikes all in one table.
Each class has its own Talent table. See the individual class files for tables.

**Humans** roll on the Talent table twice at 1st level instead of once.
All other ancestries roll once at 1st level.

## Character Tiers

Levels naturally group into tiers of play — each tier brings new titles and narrative weight:

| Levels | Tier |
|---|---|
| 1–4  | Novice       |
| 5–7  | Champion     |
| 8–10 | Master       |
