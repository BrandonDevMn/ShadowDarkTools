# Light and Darkness

## The Core Rule

Light sources burn in **real-world time**. When the group enters the dungeon, start a real timer.
When the timer expires in real life, the torch goes out in the game.

This is Shadowdark's signature mechanic. It creates relentless time pressure and forces fast decisions.

## Light Sources

| Source | Duration | Radius |
|---|---|---|
| Torch              | 1 real hour      | Near (≈30 ft) |
| Lantern            | 1 real hour per flask of oil | Double-Near (≈60 ft) |
| Oil flask (ignited, thrown/poured) | 4 rounds | Close area; 1d4 fire damage per round |
| Light spell        | 1 real hour      | Near (≈30 ft) |

## No Darkvision

**No player character ancestry can see in the dark.** This is absolute — no class, ancestry, or core feature
changes this. Monsters, however, frequently see in darkness.

## Being in Darkness

- All attacks against **unseen** targets are made with **disadvantage**
- The GM checks for random encounters more frequently in total darkness
- Darkness creates urgency and shifts the tone — the game is more dangerous and unpredictable

## Torch Notes

- Cost: **5 sp** each; takes **1 gear slot** for a bundle
- Lighting a torch in a calm situation requires no roll
- Torches can be extinguished by strong wind, water, or deliberate enemy action
- Lanterns require **oil flasks** (2 sp each) as fuel — more expensive per hour but better range and wind-resistant flame

## Why This Matters for the App

A real-time torch timer is a natural feature for a phone companion app.
Track remaining light per torch/lantern and alert the party before darkness hits.
