# Spellcasting

## Who Can Cast

- **Priests** cast using their **WIS modifier**
- **Wizards** cast using their **INT modifier**

There are no spell slots. Casters may attempt any known spell any number of times per day —
but a failed cast loses the spell until rest.

## The Casting Roll

Roll **1d20 + spellcasting modifier** vs. **DC 10 + spell tier**

| Spell Tier | DC |
|---|---|
| 1 | 11 |
| 2 | 12 |
| 3 | 13 |
| 4 | 14 |
| 5 | 15 |

## Outcomes

| Result | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Meet or beat DC | **Success** — spell works as written |
| Below DC (not a 1) | **Failure** — spell is lost until rest; cannot be attempted again until then |
| Natural 20 | **Critical success** — spell works and one numerical effect is doubled (damage dice, duration, etc.) |
| Natural 1 — Wizard | **Critical failure** — spell lost until rest; also roll on the **Mishap Table** for that spell's tier |
| Natural 1 — Priest | **Critical failure** — spell lost until rest; deity revokes access; requires **ritual penance** before recovery |

## Focus Spells (Concentration)

Some spells are **focus spells** — they require ongoing concentration to maintain.
- A caster cannot maintain more than one focus spell at a time
- At the start of each turn, make a **DC 15 INT check** to continue the spell
- Failure ends the spell immediately

## Spells Known

Casters learn a fixed number of spells per tier as they level up.
See each class file for the spells-known table by level.

- **Wizards** may add spells by studying a scroll for one day and making a **DC 15 INT check**. On success the spell is permanently added to their known spells.
- **Priests** cannot learn spells from scrolls; their spells are granted by their deity.

## Rest and Recovery

All spells lost due to failed casting are recovered after a **rest**.
Priests whose spells were revoked by their deity must additionally perform **ritual penance** — a narrative act determined by the GM — before those spells return.
